# Governance

Tenvra is company-owned and community-built. Open source participation does not automatically grant
equity, employment, payment, or legal ownership of the service or trademark.

## Roles

- **Founder/Owner:** product direction, legal responsibility, budgets, and final release authority.
- **Maintainer:** merges code, operates releases, and enforces technical standards.
- **Reviewer:** reviews scoped changes without release authority.
- **Contributor:** submits code, research, documentation, testing, or verified compute.
- **Community member:** participates in discussion and advisory votes.

## Decisions

- Routine implementation decisions use pull requests.
- Durable technical decisions use Architecture Decision Records.
- Protocol, economic, trust, licensing, or governance changes require a public RFC.
- Community votes are advisory. The company remains responsible for legal, financial, security, and
  operational decisions.

## Transparency

The project will maintain a decision log, roadmap, monthly economic reports after monetization, and
quarterly transparency reports once operations begin.

## Conflicts Of Interest

Maintainers and reviewers must disclose financial, employment, vendor, or model interests relevant
to a decision.
